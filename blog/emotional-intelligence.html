Emotional Intelligence

What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence refers to one’s ability to identify and manage one’s own emotions, as well as recognize and influence the emotions of people around them.

How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) refers o one’s intellectual ability. It measures how well a person performs in the following:

  • The ability to use logic to solve problems
  • Planning and strategizing
  • Understanding abstract ideas
  • How well they can learn and adapt to varying conditions
  • Grasping and using language

EQ (Emotional Quotient) generally refers to your ability to sense emotion in yourself and in other people.
People with high EQ finds it easier to

  • Identify emotions in themselves and others
  • Empathize with others
  • Adapt their feelings depending on the situation
  • Control their impulses
  • Withstand temptations
  • Delay gratification
  • Resolve conflicts with others
  • Communicate effectively with others

Why is EQ important?

Having a good Emotional Quotient is an important factor in how we deal with things life throws at us. We may face several stressful situations in life like conflict, change and obstacles. Understanding the situation and having control over our emotions will help in dealing with obstacles in the most sensible way.
